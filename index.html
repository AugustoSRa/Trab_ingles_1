Essential Information

The largest city in Western Europe and the EU, London is a diverse, vibrant, multicultural hub of frenetic energy; an exciting city by all accounts, that boasts some of the world’s best sights and attractions.

Situated on the River Thames, the city is divided into 32 boroughs plus the City of London, and presents an eclectic mix of the modern and the historical. Steeped in culture, music, fashion, finance and exhilarating history, London is one of the world’s ‘global cities’ and even a short trip will reveal why.

Currency:

Pound. £1 = 100 pence.

Notes come in denominations of £5, £10, £20 and £50. Coins come in denominations of 1p, 2p, 5p, 10p, 20p, 50p, £1 and £2.

Electricity:

Electricity UK plugs have three square pins. The standard electricity supply in Britain is 230V but can be slightly higher. For compatible devices, you will probably need to use an adapter, which are easily found at airports or high street shops like Argos. For devices working on a lower voltage, however, you will need a converter, look for a dual-voltage (110v-240v). An idiosyncrasy of UK sockets, is that they come with a switch. So check that you have switched on any sockets you are using

Languages:

Languages Unsurprisingly, the main spoken language is English; however London is the most linguistically diverse city in the world, with over 250 languages spoken! So whereas you may find that shopkeepers, taxi drivers and bus drivers speak only English, the tourist industry is well-equipped to welcome you in many languages. Information centers will have teams speaking at least 10 languages and there are tour guides available in over 40!

Internet:

Internet London is very well connected and has a huge range of Internet cafes, providing internet access (plus photocopy and fax facilities at some places) for about £1 per hour. Or if you have your own device, you can access Wi-Fi almost anywhere at the plethora of coffee shops, restaurants and even some tube stations. The number of hotspots in London is growing, including O2 hotspots around Oxford Street,Regent Street, and many others. O2's website offers a searchable list of over 360 hotspots available in and around the London area lists all the local cafes, pubs, restaurants and more at which you can connect.

Opening Hours:

Many large supermarkets are open 24 hours from Sunday midnight to Saturday midnight and then again during the day on Sunday. Museum and attraction opening times are typically 10 a.m. to 5.30 p.m. every day, with some opening earlier and others closing later. DAY TWO

Starting our day, we will take an Uber from Nobu Hotel to the Sherlock Holmes Museum, where we will spend a few hours. Afterward, we will explore the nearby attractions on foot, such as Madame Tussauds Museum, Regent's Park, and Fisher's. These sites are located in proximity to each other.

On the third day, we will once again travel by car to visit the iconic London Bridge and explore the chilling atmosphere of the Jack the Ripper Museum, which is conveniently located nearby. DAY FOUR

On the 4th day, we will on a car to explore the renowned Science Museum and the fascinating Natural History Museum. After immersing ourselves in the wonders of science and nature, we will head to the nearby restaurant, Stein's Berlin, to enjoy a delicious meal.

DAY FIVE

Starting on the 5th day, we will resume our car journey and head towards the Royal Observatory Greenwich. This iconic location offers fascinating astronomy activities and is home to the famous Prime Meridian line. After immersing ourselves in the celestial wonders, we will proceed to the National Maritime Museum and the Old Royal Naval College to delve deeper into England's rich naval history. To conclude our day, we will visit the nearby Midpoint Restaurant, where we can indulge in a satisfying meal.

On the 6th day, we will continue our car journey in London, starting with a visit to Kensington Palace, exploring its lavish rooms and beautiful gardens. Afterwards, we will make our way to the iconic Royal Albert Hall for a guided tour, learning about its rich musical heritage. We will then conclude the day by visiting the Serpentine Gallery, immersing ourselves in contemporary art exhibitions.

On the 7th day, we will visit Flourish Craft Bakery for artisanal breads and pastries. Next, we'll explore the Harry Potter Studio Tour to discover the iconic sets and costumes from the films. Later, we'll take a break at Tim Hortons to enjoy their renowned coffee and treats. It will be a day filled with culinary delights, enchantment, and relaxation.

DAY EIGHT

On the 8th day, we will continue our exploration of London's iconic landmarks. Our first stop will be the renowned Big Ben, where we will admire the majestic clock tower and take in the impressive architecture of the surrounding area. Next, we will visit Westminster Hall, a historic and significant building that has witnessed centuries of British history. From there, we will make our way to the Palace of Westminster, home to the Houses of Parliament and known for its striking Gothic Revival style. As we explore these iconic sites, we will immerse ourselves in the rich history and political significance that they represent.

DAY NINE

On the 9th day, we will continue our exploration of London's cultural scene. We will start at the Tate Modern, a renowned contemporary art museum showcasing diverse artworks. Next, we will visit the Shakespeare's Globe, a reconstruction of the iconic Elizabethan theater, where we can witness live performances of Shakespearean plays. After that, we will head to the Menier Chocolate Factory, a unique venue combining a theater and restaurant, offering a delightful combination of food and performances. Finally, we will conclude the day at Casa do Frango, a popular Portuguese restaurant, where we can indulge in traditional cuisine.

DAY TEN

On the 10th day, we will visit the Freud Museum, dedicated to the life and work of Sigmund Freud, exploring his influential contributions to psychology. Afterwards, we will make our way to the London Zoo, where we can observe a wide variety of animal species and learn about wildlife conservation.

DAY ELEVEN

On the 11th day, we will begin our exploration at the Chelsea Physic Garden, a historic botanical garden dedicated to the study of medicinal plants. Here, we can learn about the diverse plant species and their practical applications. Next, we will visit the National Army Museum, where we can delve into the rich military history of Britain and explore the exhibits showcasing the army's role throughout the centuries. If the timing aligns, we may have the opportunity to attend the prestigious Chelsea Flower Show, a renowned event showcasing stunning floral displays and innovative gardening techniques. To conclude the day, we will head to Mezcalito Chelsea, a bar known for, offering a relaxing and enjoyable evening.

DAY TWELVE

On the 12th day, our exploration continues with a visit to St. Paul's Cathedral, an iconic architectural masterpiece with its impressive dome and rich history. We will admire the grandeur of the cathedral and, if desired, explore the interior for its stunning artwork and intricate design. Next, we will make our way to Inner Temple Gardens, a tranquil oasis nestled in the heart of the city, offering a serene escape from the bustling streets. We can take a leisurely stroll through the well-manicured gardens, appreciating the beauty of the surroundings. Lastly, we will cross the Millenium Bridge, a pedestrian bridge that spans the River Thames, offering panoramic views of the city skyline.

DAY THIRTEEN

On the 13th day, we will explore some of London's popular attractions. We will begin at the Sea Life London Aquarium, where we can observe a variety of marine species. Next, we will visit DreamWork's Tours Shrek's Adventure! London, an interactive experience based on the beloved animated film. We will then head to the London Dungeon, an immersive attraction showcasing the city's dark history. As night falls, we will conclude our day with a visit to the London Eye, offering panoramic views of the city skyline. It will be a day filled with diverse attractions and opportunities to explore different aspects of London's entertainment scene.

DAY FOURTEEN

On the final day of our journey, we will make our way back to Votuporanga from London. We will check out of our accommodation and head to Heathrow Airport for our return flight. After landing in Votuporanga, we will retrieve our luggage and make our way home, reflecting on the experiences and memories we have made during our time in London. It will mark the end of our trip, and we will carry with us the stories and photos to share with others.

Transportation between activities follows a specific pattern. We leave the Nobu Hotel by car and drive to the first activity of the day. However, for the remaining activities, we explore on foot. Each activity was carefully selected and arranged to allow us to walk between locations and experience the entire city. This combination of car and walking ensures that we can efficiently reach our destinations and fully immerse ourselves in the city's sights and sounds. By embracing this approach, we have the opportunity to appreciate the city's beauty and discover hidden gems that we might have otherwise missed.
